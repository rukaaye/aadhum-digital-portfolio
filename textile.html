Welcome to the intersection of tradition and technology. A Little Bit About Me

Hello World. My name is Ruka, a first-generation Ethiopian-American and the eldest of five. I've always had the privilege of direct access to my culture. My earliest connection to my heritage was through music, followed by language, and then back to music videos. I was fascinated by the different outfits and jewelry each region would showcase. This was the foundation… Fast forward to today, I am a cultural contemporary designer specializing in Ethiopian culture and materials. I’ve learned to fully embrace both my Ethiopian and American identities. There were times when I felt conflicted about my American side because I longed to be fully connected to my African ancestors. However, AADHum helped me see the power and strength that lie within this duality. My strong foundation in my culture gave me the courage to create my own adaptations, which began gaining attention. This recognition encouraged me to further invest my intentions into this field, solidifying my purpose in this work.

Textile + Computer: What Connects Them?

The key is a microcontroller called the “Adafruit GEMMA M0.”

This microcontroller connects to a computer via conductive wires. The wires not only link the microcontroller to the computer but also bring your code to life on a touch-sensitive interface. By simply touching different parts of the microcontroller, you can activate various sections of your code, making the entire system interactive!
